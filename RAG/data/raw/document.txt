Comprehensive Assessment Guidelines for School Infrastructure in Nepal
 (Based on National Standards)

These guidelines provide a structured framework for assessing school infrastructure in Nepal, with reference to national standards and best practices outlined in official documents. The evaluation covers several critical areas, ensuring that school facilities are safe, accessible, and conducive to learning.
1. Safety and Structural Integrity
Objective: Assess the physical condition of school buildings, their compliance with national building codes, and their ability to withstand natural hazards such as earthquakes, floods, and storms.
Standards and Guidelines:
●	Structural Stability: Buildings must comply with the Nepal National Building Code (NBC 105: Seismic Design) for earthquake resistance.
●	Condition: Inspect for cracks, tilting, material deterioration, and visible structural damage.
●	Retrofitting: Determine if retrofitting or repairs are required based on the building's age and exposure to seismic risk.
●	Safety Features: Presence of fire exits, extinguishers, alarm systems, and accessibility features for emergency evacuation.
Rating: 1 to 5 (1 = Critical, 5 = Excellent)
2. Space and Capacity
Objective: Ensure that the school provides sufficient space for students to learn comfortably, in line with student-teacher ratios and classroom size guidelines.
Standards and Guidelines:
●	Classroom Size: Minimum of 1.1 square meters per student. Classrooms should be at least 6.7m x 5.5m with a minimum ceiling height of 3 meters.
●	Overcrowding: Inspect classrooms to ensure they are not overcrowded, with no more than 40 students per classroom.
●	Flexible Learning Spaces: Schools should have multipurpose rooms for flexible learning activities.
Rating: 1 to 5 (1 = Overcrowded and insufficient space, 5 = Adequate space and flexible learning environments)
3. Learning Environment Quality
Objective: Assess the quality of the learning environment in terms of lighting, ventilation, and furniture that enhances student comfort and concentration.
Standards and Guidelines: 
●	Lighting: Classrooms should have ample natural light and proper ventilation, with windows covering at least 20% of the floor area.
●	Furniture: Desks and benches should be age-appropriate and ergonomic, ensuring comfort for extended use.
●	Ventilation and Climate Control: Classrooms should be well-ventilated, and climate control systems (heating or cooling) should be functional where necessary.
Rating: 1 to 5 (1 = Poor lighting and ventilation, 5 = Excellent environment for learning)
4. Accessibility and Inclusivity
Objective: Evaluate the school's compliance with accessibility standards to ensure that all students, including those with disabilities, can access and use the facilities.
Standards and Guidelines:
●	Universal Design: Buildings must have ramps, handrails, and wide doorways to accommodate students with physical disabilities.
●	Gender Sensitivity: Separate toilets for boys and girls are required, with accommodations for menstrual hygiene management for female students.
●	Inclusive Facilities: Classrooms should include aids and facilities for students with special needs, including tactile surfaces and signage in Braille.
Rating: 1 to 5 (1 = No accessible facilities, 5 = Fully accessible and inclusive)
5. Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene (WASH) Facilities
Objective: Ensure the availability of clean drinking water, sanitation facilities, and handwashing stations in line with national health and safety standards.
Standards and Guidelines:
●	Safe Water Supply: Schools should provide a reliable source of potable drinking water.
●	Toilets: The recommended ratio is 1 toilet per 50 students, with separate toilets for boys and girls. Toilets should be clean, ventilated, and well-maintained.
●	Handwashing Facilities: Schools must provide functional handwashing stations with soap.
Rating: 1 to 5 (1 = No clean water or sanitation, 5 = Excellent WASH facilities)
6. Sustainability and Environmental Factors
Objective: Evaluate the school’s environmental sustainability in terms of energy efficiency, use of sustainable construction materials, and water conservation practices.
Standards and Guidelines:
●	Energy Efficiency: Schools should use energy-efficient lighting and appliances, and where possible, renewable energy sources such as solar panels.
●	Sustainable Materials: Buildings should be constructed with eco-friendly materials, minimizing their environmental footprint.
●	Water Conservation: Schools should implement water-saving measures, such as rainwater harvesting and efficient plumbing systems.
Rating: 1 to 5 (1 = No sustainable practices, 5 = Highly sustainable and environmentally friendly)
7. Technology and Connectivity
Objective: Assess the availability of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) infrastructure and internet connectivity to support digital learning.
Standards and Guidelines:
●	ICT Infrastructure: Schools should have functional computer labs, smart classrooms, and reliable internet access.
●	Future-Proofing: Schools should be equipped with sufficient outlets, data cabling, and adaptable spaces for future technological advancements.
Rating: 1 to 5 (1 = No ICT infrastructure, 5 = Fully equipped and connected)
8. Health and Hygiene
Objective: Evaluate the cleanliness of the school, availability of health services, and air quality to ensure a healthy learning environment.
Standards and Guidelines:
●	Cleanliness: Schools should have regular cleaning schedules and pest control systems.
●	Health Services: Availability of first-aid rooms or access to nearby health clinics.
●	Air Quality: Classrooms should have good air quality, with proper ventilation and use of non-toxic materials.
Rating: 1 to 5 (1 = Unhealthy environment, 5 = Clean and hygienic)
9. Disaster Risk Reduction and Climate Resilience
Objective: Ensure that the school infrastructure is designed to withstand natural disasters and that disaster preparedness plans are in place.
Standards and Guidelines:
●	Disaster-Resilient Buildings: Compliance with NBC standards for earthquake and flood-resistant construction.
●	Disaster Preparedness: Schools must have evacuation plans, emergency drills, and access to emergency kits.
●	Climate Adaptation: Schools in vulnerable areas should be designed to withstand extreme weather events, with climate-resilient features like elevated buildings in flood-prone areas.
Rating: 1 to 5 (1 = No disaster preparedness, 5 = Highly resilient and prepared)
10. Adaptability for Future Growth
Objective: Assess the school’s capacity for future expansion and adaptability to new educational methods or increased student numbers.
Standards and Guidelines:
●	Scalability: Schools should have space for additional classrooms, laboratories, or other facilities.
●	Flexible Design: Buildings should allow for modification to accommodate future educational needs, such as collaborative learning spaces.
Rating: 1 to 5 (1 = No room for expansion, 5 = Fully adaptable and scalable)

Scoring Summary:
Each category is scored from 1 to 5, with a total maximum score of 50. The overall score will help identify priority areas for improvement and guide resource allocation for school infrastructure development in Nepal.
